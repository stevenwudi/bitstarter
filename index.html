This brilliant idea was formalized during my summer internship at the AMAZING Microsoft Research Cambridge and a talk with the smart young lady Abigail See (left photo, lol, need copy right for the photo). One day before lunch time, Abi talked about the idea: “Isn’t quite fucking annoying that someone wants talk to you whilst you are listening the music through earphone and you need to take off the earphone and press the stop button of your mp3 or ipod or whatever. It wastes both the speaker and yourself sometime, I wish I could have a cool device that can stop automatically when I take off my earphone.” And I was like: “Hmm, it is such a multi-million pound idea and it’s odd that no one actually think about it. Weird, ini’t ? (I was thinking in a Sheffield Yorkshire accent).

After telling my close colleages about the Insteaph (fictional story begins...), the Dutch Jeori and the German Jakob decided to go to Wales for the business meeting. Sitting in a welsh pub, they were plotting whether they should plagiarize this multi-million pound idea. Jakob was pouting because the snobbish plus slightly posh German Jakob considered the idea was too immature and naïve. Hence Jakob rejected the budding idea with contempt. Meanwhile, the Dutch Jeori pretended to be a visionary, and was looking profoundly. However, all he was thinking about is when he can have the fudge from Sheffield!

Knowing I wouldn’t be able to raise any start up investment from The Dutch and The German, I naturally came to my neighbour colleague: The Italiano Matteo. At first, he frown upon for a while, saying: “you should anticipate” (a Kinect tennis insider joke). As a Chinese entrepreneur, I could not phantom what does his “anticipate” mean. Then, the quintessential Italiano, who come from a nation that has a mafia kidnapped the smallest pony, resolutely refused my proposal of Insteaph: “Nah nah nah, you people put pineapple together with pizza, WTF, FTS (apologize for the over dramatized vulgar language).” The always friendly Itanliano suddenly went rampage when it comes to pizza with l’ananas. Without even having a look at the prototype of the product, I got a cold-shoulder treatment from the warm Italiano.

Then I remember the always amiable Cartier, she must be willing a lend me a hand about this brilliantly conceived start up project.

Nonetheless,

someone suddenly poped up from the dark corner.

Jakob: "What I have told you, is NOT gonna work!"

The almighty German eyes serve as an intimitating command.

Cartier, timidly: "Nody sees this, nody sees you have talked to me.”

I was like, yeah, right. Nobody sees somebody bumped into a glass door after two months, and nobody sees somebody slipped and fell over a bench in a very unladylike manner. (Please condone me for crossing the line by telling those hilarious anecdotes, cher amie K).

Suddenly, in this little tavern of this remote Welsh bar, a familiar Indian with a perfect Punjabi accent comes around:

“Yo BRO!”

(Before the story goes on, a voice-over from off screen: OMG, Bhaskar, why your facebook fotos are all so inharmoniously big! story continues...)

“Aha BRO!” I am thrilled to see my bro here and obviously, we have different mothers.

“I will be the angel investment of your start-up and I will always got your back!”

Just like this, the start-up project of Insteaph found an angel investment. And the prince and princess lived happily after…
